Scene Setup

In an industrial park on the outskirts of a Philippines prefecture town, factories, storage tanks, and low-rise concrete buildings fill the landscape. Utility poles, narrow roads, and scattered warehouses create a grounded, everyday setting.

Both @image1 and @image2 are 50 meters tall, towering over the environment. Their massive scale feels heavy and physical—every step cracks asphalt, bends steel, and sends dust into the air. Smoke rises from already damaged structures.

Visual Style

Shot in classic tokusatsu filming style with realistic natural visuals.
Ground-level camera perspective emphasizes scale—slight handheld shake, practical lighting, and real-world textures.
No stylized effects—only physical destruction, dust, debris, and impact.
Hyper-detailed skin texture, hair strands with visible pores and lifelike fabric and surface detail, HDR lighting, natural shadows.

Action (Single Continuous Feel)

Opening
Low-angle ground shot looking up between buildings.
@image1 steps forward, each footfall shaking the ground. Nearby structures rattle, windows crack, and debris falls.

Engagement
@image2 charges in. The camera tracks from street level as both giants collide.
@image1 throws a heavy punch—slow but powerful—connecting with @image2’s torso. The impact sends a visible shock through the body, pushing @image2 backward into a warehouse, crushing part of it.

Mid-Fight
The camera moves alongside them at ground level, weaving between debris and damaged vehicles.
@image2 tries to counter, swinging back, but @image1 blocks and responds with repeated, relentless punches. Each hit produces bursts of dust, sparks, and structural collapse in the background.

Climax
@image1 steps in, rotates his body, and delivers a powerful kick.
The kick lands cleanly, knocking @image2 off balance. The massive body crashes into multiple buildings, collapsing them in sequence with thick smoke rising.

Ending Shot
Ground-level camera slowly tilts up through drifting smoke.
@image2 lies fallen among wreckage.
@image1 stands firm, breathing heavily, surrounded by destruction.

Sound Design

No dialogue.
Only heavy impacts, metal bending, debris crashing, distant alarms, and deep rumbling footsteps.
Wind carries smoke and dust, reinforcing the grounded, realistic tokusatsu atmosphere.
No logos, no text, no subtitles, no cartoonish.
